Care and Handling instructions for Ready to Wear Garments: and leave the rest as is.

The hand knit garment you have purchased from made to is 100% natural fibers, therefore requires an extra bit of care. If you are not accustomed to this process, please give it a try, as it’s very simple. All of our garments can also be put out for dry cleaning.

Step 1. Fill a basin (sink, bathtub, etc) full of warm tap water (no hotter than 40C). add your favorite no-rinse wool wash to the basin and swirl gently.

Step 2. Place your item into the basin and without agitating, press down on the garment so water soaks into the fibers. If there are any spills or spots on the garment, rub very gently to dissolve.

Step 3. Let the garment soak for about an hour. This will allow any grit to fall out of the fibers, and into the bottom of the basin

Step 4. After the allowed soaking time, take the garment out of the basin and gently squeeze the excess water out. If you have a top loading washing machine, using the spin cycle is a good way to help dry the garment. If the spin cycle is not an option, rolling the garment in a towel and squeezing a second time is also an option.

Step 5. After all excess water has been squeezed out, depending on the garment, lay flat or hang to dry.

Step 6. For an extra crisp finish, press the garment with a steam iron (pressing and lifting only, not pushing or dragging the iron across the fabric) Use the iron to press out any marks left by hangers or any stretching you feel has occurred.

I hope these instructions were helpful.
